# V4 Revision Strategy: The "Safety Net" Synthesis

## Goal
To create a version of the paper that is **theologically orthodox** (retaining the V3 core) but **politically bulletproof** (wrapped in the language of official Chinese theology).

## The Synthesis Matrix

| V3 Core Concept (Orthodoxy) | Official Theology "Safety Net" (Cover) | Synthesis Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Common Grace** (Calvin) | **Cosmic Christ** (K.H. Ting) | Explicitly parallel the two. Argument: Calvin's "Common Grace" finds its contemporary Chinese expression in Bishop Ting's "Cosmic Christ" — both affirm truth/goodness outside the church. |
| **Antithesis / Sanctification** | **Personality / Character** (T.C. Chao) | Use Chao's "Personality" (人格) to describe the *goal* of Sanctification. The "New Man" in Christ is the fulfillment of the Confucian ideal of "Personality". |
| **Two Kingdoms / Public Witness** | **Social Gospel / Mutual Supplement** (Y.T. Wu) | Reference Wu's "Mutual Supplement" (between faith and materialism/society) as a localized application of the "Two Kingdoms" — serving society without losing distinct identity. |

## Detailed Edits by Section

### Abstract
- **Add**: Mention that this reconstruction aligns with the "Theological Construction" envisioned by Bishop Ting.

### Section 2: Demystification (Theology)
- **Modify**: When introducing "Common Grace", immediately cite **Bishop Ting's "Cosmic Christ"** as a resonant concept.
- **Quote**: Use Ting's quote: "The truth, goodness, and beauty outside the church are also the result of Christ's immanence." (教外的真善美也是基督内在与圣灵圣化的结果).
- **Effect**: This legitimizes "Common Grace" not just as a Western Calvinist idea, but as a continuation of the Three-Self theological tradition.

### Section 3: Core Construction (Public Witness)
- **Modify**: When discussing "Public Witness", interpret it through **T.C. Chao's "Personality" (人格)**.
- **Argument**: The "Public Witness" is the manifestation of a transformed "Christian Personality". This appeals to the Chinese intellectual tradition's focus on character.

### Section 4: Practice (Ethics)
- **Add**: Acknowledging **Y.T. Wu's** contribution to contextualizing Christianity in a socialist society. Frame the "Two Kingdoms" responsibility as carrying forward the "Patriotic" tradition of the Three-Self founders.

## Risk Assessment
- **Risk**: Diluting the "Antithesis" (Sola Gratia) by over-associating with Ting's "Love" theology (which tends to universalism).
- **Mitigation**: Keep the V3 distinction: These are *Common Grace* (Providential), not *Saving Grace*. Use Ting to validate the *existence* of goodness outside the church, but retain Calvin to define its *limit*.
