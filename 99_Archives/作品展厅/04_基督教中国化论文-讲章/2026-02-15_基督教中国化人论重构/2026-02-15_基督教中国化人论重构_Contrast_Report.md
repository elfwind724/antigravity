# Comparative Analysis: Two Roads for Chinese Theology

## Overview
You now have two distinct versions of the paper, representing two fundamentally different theological strategies.

### V4: The "Safety Net" Synthesis (The Priestly Approach)
*   **Strategy**: **Adaptation & Synthesis**.
*   **Core Logic**: Use official theological language (Ting, Chao, Wu) to wrap orthodox doctrines.
*   **Key Concept**: "Cosmic Christ" (common grace) & "Personality" (sanctification).
*   **Tone**: Constructive, harmonizing, safe.
*   **Target Audience**: Official seminaries (Jinling), government religious affairs departments, public intellectuals.
*   **Risk**: Theological compromise (Universalism), loss of distinctiveness.

### V5: The Prophetic Critique (The Prophetic Approach)
*   **Strategy**: **Antithesis & Judgment**.
*   **Core Logic**: "Sola Scriptura" as a sword to judge culture. Reject all synthesis.
*   **Key Concept**: "Total Depravity" & "Radical Rebirth".
*   **Tone**: Sharp, offensive (Gal 5:11), uncompromising.
*   **Target Audience**: House churches, serious theological students, those disillusioned with "cultural Christianity".
*   **Risk**: Political danger (seen as "uncooperative"), marginalization.

## The Choice
*   **Choose V4** if you want to **survive and influence form within** the system.
*   **Choose V5** if you want to **speak truth to power** regardless of the cost.
